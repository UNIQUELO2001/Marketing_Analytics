# Analytics Delivery Process

## Overview

This document outlines the methodology used to deliver analytics projects from initial business understanding to actionable recommendations.

The framework is designed to ensure that every dashboard is aligned with business objectives and supports data-driven decision-making.

---

# Project Workflow

```text
Business Understanding
        │
        ▼
Requirements Gathering
        │
        ▼
Data Exploration
        │
        ▼
Data Modeling
        │
        ▼
Dashboard Development
        │
        ▼
Insight Generation
        │
        ▼
Business Recommendations
        │
        ▼
Project Delivery
```

---

# Phase 1 — Business Understanding

## Objective

Understand the business before writing a single SQL query.

### Questions

- What problem needs to be solved?
- Who will use the dashboard?
- What decisions will this dashboard support?
- Which KPIs matter most?

### Deliverables

- Business Problem Statement
- Project Objectives
- Success Metrics

---

# Phase 2 — Requirements Gathering

## Objective

Translate business questions into measurable metrics.

### Example

| Business Question | KPI |
|-------------------|-----|
| How much revenue are we generating? | Revenue |
| Which channels perform best? | Revenue by Source |
| Where are customers dropping off? | Funnel Conversion Rate |

### Deliverables

- KPI List
- Dashboard Requirements
- Data Requirements

---

# Phase 3 — Data Exploration

## Objective

Understand the available data before building reports.

### Activities

- Review available tables
- Understand event structure
- Validate data quality
- Identify required dimensions
- Identify required metrics

### Deliverables

- Data Inventory
- Entity Relationships
- Initial Findings

---

# Phase 4 — Data Modeling

## Objective

Transform raw event-level data into business-ready datasets.

### Activities

- Build SQL queries
- Aggregate KPIs
- Create reporting tables
- Validate calculations

### Deliverables

- SQL Queries
- Reporting Tables
- KPI Definitions

---

# Phase 5 — Dashboard Development

## Objective

Design dashboards that answer business questions clearly.

### Principles

- Simplicity
- Consistency
- Executive Readability
- Business Focus

### Dashboard Structure

1. Executive Overview
2. Marketing Performance
3. Funnel Analysis

### Deliverables

- Dashboard Layout
- KPI Cards
- Interactive Reports

---

# Phase 6 — Insight Generation

## Objective

Transform data into business insights.

### Focus Areas

- Trends
- Opportunities
- Risks
- Performance Gaps
- Growth Opportunities

### Deliverables

- Executive Summary
- Business Findings
- Supporting Analysis

---

# Phase 7 — Recommendations

## Objective

Provide practical, prioritized recommendations.

Recommendations should be:

- Actionable
- Measurable
- Business-focused
- Prioritized by impact

### Example

| Priority | Recommendation |
|----------|----------------|
| High | Optimize checkout completion |
| High | Improve product discovery |
| Medium | Increase investment in high-performing channels |

---

# Phase 8 — Project Delivery

## Final Deliverables

- Executive Dashboard
- Marketing Dashboard
- Funnel Dashboard
- SQL Documentation
- Business Insights
- Case Study Documentation

---

# Design Principles

Every analytics project should follow these principles.

## Business First

Understand the business problem before building technical solutions.

---

## Simplicity

Present only the metrics that support decision-making.

---

## Accuracy

Validate calculations and ensure consistency across reports.

---

## Actionability

Every dashboard should lead to business decisions, not just observations.

---

## Scalability

Design reports and SQL queries that can be extended as business requirements evolve.

---

# Quality Assurance Checklist

Before delivering a project, verify that:

- Business objectives have been addressed.
- KPIs are validated.
- SQL queries return expected results.
- Dashboard filters function correctly.
- Visualizations are easy to understand.
- Insights are supported by data.
- Recommendations are actionable.

---

# Continuous Improvement

Every completed project should contribute to improving future engagements.

Areas for continuous improvement include:

- SQL optimization
- Dashboard usability
- Automation
- Documentation
- Data quality
- Business storytelling

---

# Methodology Summary

This framework provides a repeatable process for delivering analytics projects that combine technical implementation with business strategy.

The goal is not only to build dashboards, but to create reporting solutions that help organizations make informed decisions, improve operational performance, and drive sustainable growth.
